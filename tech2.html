IT Projects

The age of fast paced technologies and connectivity

Dear Reader,

I would like to start by thanking you for taking the time to look at my projects. We are in a time when human progress has never been as fast as it is today. Whether it's shorter product life cycles, faster deliveries, becoming rich through social media, or groundbreaking discoveries in medicine or physics. It's all made possible because of the internet. It allows people and machines to connect is changing the way we communicate with each other today and in the future. I am curious to see where the journey goes.

Data Science course

Since I wanted to expand my IT knowledge with another programming language and Python is one of the most popular for processing and using data, I took a voluntary course last summer, which I successfully passed. The course includes fundamentals from data science. In particular, the focus is on data acquisition, preparation, description and visualization with the Python programming language. As can be seen below, there were a lot of exciting exercises involved and I also tried it out privately with publicly available datasets. Although I did a full time internship during the time and wrote several exams at times, I enjoyed every second and I will definitely add advanced courses to it.

An app for the university canteen

My first contact with HTML and mobile approach was during my bachelor's degree in a voluntary course on mobile service business. The content of the lecture first deals with the basics and manifestations of mobile business. Subsequently, the areas of mobile technologies, mobile services, mobile commerce and mobile customer relationship management are dealt with. The framework topic Mobile HealthCare gives an insight into the use of mobile services in healthcare and the potential of mobile technologies. As part of the course, I was able to create a mobile app for the university cafeteria, which you can see below. (it is best to open the individual links in a new window).

Data Visualization with Tableau

I wanted to make good use of the time during the lockdown and have been learning Tableau. After the program unfortunately came a bit short in our university, I decided to learn it with Youtube. First, I downloaded the official data on corona infections from the WHO. In the next step, I visualized the top 10 countries with the highest death and infection rates in a dashboard. Unfortunately, the online embedding is not the best (I think this is due to Tableau public), which is why the play button works but doesn't speed up. Anyway, feel free to download and use the dashboard.

This Website

I worked on this website to freshen my HTML skill, work on the launch of the new Inopia website, and of course present myself in a creative way. Since I also completed an Online basic course in CSS, I also benefited from these basics. In the context of this project it was important to consider different aspects of UX and IT, but also to put myself in the reader's shoes and think of what would entertain him or her. Trial and error was the key to success and I could truly see that nowadays you can learn everything by yourself and I am looking forward to more similar future projects to get better.
